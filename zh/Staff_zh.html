2014 Staff

Kartikeya Menon

Kartik is a sophomore Math and Japanese double major from New York, NY. He is currently the Secretary for the Dartmouth Japan Society and on the editorial board for DUJS, Dartmouth's science publication, and works in a biology lab. He went on the Japanese LSA+ this past summer term and just came back from a winter internship at the U.S. Embassy in Tokyo. In his free time he likes listening to Japanese music and playing tennis.

Thomas Rover

Thomas is a Classical Archaeology Major, with a Japanese Minor. He studied Mandarin Chinese for 4 years in high school, and spent a month in Beijing studying at the Beijing Language and Culture University (BLCU) with his high school class. He went on the Japanese LSA+ this past summer term, and is currently working on finishing the Minor in Japanese.



Tangent Cheng

Tangent is a current Japanese/Economics double major who previously served as the Pan Asian Council Representative as well as Secretary for the Dartmouth Japan Society. He is also currently on the executive board of the Students of Hong Kong. He went on the Japan LSA+ last summer, and will be doing an exchange term to Keio University in Tokyo this coming fall. He also spoke at, assisted in, and attended a Japan-related conference organized by the Dartmouth Japanese Department in the past fall. He was born in Hong Kong and is fluent in Cantonese Chinese and proficient in Japanese and Mandarin Chinese. He enjoys listening to Japanese music and watching Japanese animation in his spare time.

Angela Jin

Angela is a Biology and Psychology double major, as well as a pre-veterinary student, from NJ. On campus she is heavily involved in many organizations, such as the Dartmouth College Wind Ensemble, World Outlook, the Dartmouth, etc. She is currently, among other things, President of the Students of Hong Kong, Vice-President of the Dartmouth Chinese Cultural Society, President of the Society of Biological Sciences, and Co-Chair of Animal Care Enthusiasts. As an American-born Chinese, she is proficient in Mandarin Chinese, and went on the Music Foreign Study Progam to Beijing, China in Spring 2013. Additionally she participates in research in two biology labs, volunteers at the local animal shelter, and enjoys listening to Korean pop and cooking.



Yoon Kim

Yoon is a sophomore from Tokyo, Japan majoring in Economics and Neuroscience. She now lives in Seoul, Korea and is heavily involved with Korean Students Association, International Students Association, and Kappa Kappa Gamma sorority on campus.

Claire Park

Claire Park is a '16 from Oregon studying chemistry and government. She was born in South Korea and moved to the U.S. with her parents at the age of four. Fluent in Korean, she recently had a cross-cultural internship in a biopharmacy lab in Seoul. Wesley Lau

Wesley Lau '15 lives in Seattle, WA. He is a double major in Japanese and Economics at Dartmouth. Wesley studied abroad in Japan through Dartmouth's LSA+ program during the summer of 2012 and worked with Professor Dorsey in Fall 2013 with his research on Japanese folk music and protest in the 1970s as part of the Presidential Scholars program. He is currently writing his thesis on antinuclear protest after the Fukushima disaster. In his spare time he is the current co-president of the Dartmouth Japan Society and enjoys Asian cinema and fashion.
